Block A — Short definitions (Q1 answers)
30 min 10 marks secured
Crystal habit + Pinacoid + Dome
2–3 line definition each. Crystal habit = external shape developed under growth conditions. Pinacoid = open form of 2 parallel faces. Dome = 2 faces meeting at an edge parallel to one axis.
must
Twinning — Carlsbad vs Polysynthetic
Carlsbad = penetration twin in orthoclase, twin axis = c-axis. Polysynthetic = repeated contact twins in plagioclase, parallel composition planes. Both have appeared in 5 of 6 papers.
must
Tenacity + Fracture types + Cleavage
Tenacity = resistance to breaking (brittle, sectile, malleable, elastic, flexible). Fracture = irregular break, not along crystal planes (conchoidal, hackly, uneven). Cleavage = break along crystallographic planes — perfect/good/poor.
must
Idiochromatic minerals + Colour variation reasons
Idiochromatic = colour from essential element (malachite = green from Cu). Allochromatic = colour from impurities. Colour varies due to: inclusions, crystal defects, pigments, physical structure. Appeared in 5/6 papers.
must
Metallogenic epoch + Gangue + Ore grade
Metallogenic epoch = geological time interval when major ore deposits formed under specific conditions. Gangue = economically worthless minerals in an ore body. Ore grade = metal content per tonne (g/t for gold, % for base metals).
high
Steno's law + Bragg's law + Solid angle
Steno's law = interfacial angles between corresponding faces are constant for the same mineral at same temp. Bragg's law: nλ = 2d sinθ (X-ray diffraction by crystal planes). Solid angle = 3D angular space at a crystal vertex.
high
💡 Write each definition on rough paper — 2–3 sentences max. You're not writing essays, just showing you know the concept. Crisp = more marks in 2 marks questions.
Block B — Short notes (Q2 bank, pick best 4 of these 5)
30 min 20 marks secured
Coal — ranks + microscopic units (macerals)
Ranks (increasing carbon %): Peat → Lignite → Bituminous → Anthracite. Macerals: Vitrinite (woody tissue), Exinite (spores, algae, resin), Inertinite (oxidised matter). Varieties of bituminous: cannel, boghead, splint coal. Appeared in ALL 6 papers — easiest 5 marks ever.
must
Optical properties of hornblende
PPL: dark green/brown colour, strong pleochroism (green↔brown↔yellow), 2 cleavages at 124° and 56°, good relief. XPL: birefringence 0.014–0.026, 2nd order blue-green interference colours, inclined extinction ~15–25°, no twinning. Amphibole group. Appeared in 3/6 papers.
must
Chief ores of copper
Native copper, Chalcopyrite (CuFeS₂) — most important, Chalcocite (Cu₂S), Bornite (Cu₅FeS₄), Malachite (Cu₂CO₃(OH)₂), Azurite, Cuprite (Cu₂O). Indian deposits: Khetri (Rajasthan) — largest, Malanjkhand (MP), Singhbhum (Jharkhand).
must
Concordant vs discordant ore bodies
Concordant: parallel to host rock structure — beds/seams, sills, saddle reefs, mantos, lenses, stratabound. Discordant: cuts across host rock — veins, lodes, pipes, dikes, stockworks, breccia pipes, contact metasomatic. Draw a simple cross-section diagram showing both.
must
Applications of minerals in war
Titanium (armour plating, aircraft), Beryllium (nuclear reactors, X-ray windows), Mica (electrical insulators in communication equipment), Graphite (explosives, lubricants), Quartz (radio/radar oscillators), Magnetite (compass), Chromite (armour steel). Appeared in 4/6 papers.
high
💡 Each short note = ~8–10 lines in the answer book. Include a diagram for ore bodies and cleavage angles. Diagrams pull marks even if your written content is thin.
Phase 2 · 7:00 – 8:00 PM · Q3 + Q4 long answers
Block C — Crystal systems long answer (Q3)
25 min 10 marks
Hexagonal system — axes, symmetry, forms + diagram
4 axes: 3 horizontal equal (a₁=a₂=a₃) at 120° + 1 vertical (c) at 90°. Symmetry elements: 1 six-fold principal axis, 6 two-fold axes, 7 planes of symmetry, 1 centre. Forms: hexagonal prism, hexagonal pyramid, hexagonal dipyramid, pinacoid. Draw labelled axis diagram. Examples: quartz, beryl, apatite.
must
Tetragonal system — axes, symmetry, forms + diagram
3 axes: 2 equal horizontal (a₁=a₂) + 1 vertical (c≠a), all at 90°. Symmetry: 1 four-fold axis, 4 two-fold axes, 5 planes, 1 centre. Forms: tetragonal prism, tetragonal pyramid, tetragonal dipyramid, pinacoid, disphenoid. Examples: zircon, cassiterite, rutile, vesuvianite.
high
Optical properties under PPL + XPL (backup if above don't appear)
PPL: colour, pleochroism, form/habit, cleavage, relief, inclusions, alteration. XPL: birefringence (thickness × Δn), Newton's colour scale, extinction angle (straight/inclined/symmetrical), interference figure, twinning. Draw Newton's colour chart — 1st to 4th order bands.
high
💡 Crystal system answers: axis diagram + symmetry table = 6 marks minimum. The remaining 4 come from correctly naming forms and giving 2–3 mineral examples. Don't skip the diagram — it's free marks.
Block D — Economic geology long answer (Q4)
25 min 10 marks
Iron ore — formation processes + Indian distribution
Formation: BIF (banded iron formation) — sedimentary precambrian deposits, magmatic segregation (titano-magnetite), lateritic weathering (limonite). Ore minerals: magnetite (Fe₃O₄), hematite (Fe₂O₃), limonite, siderite. India: Jharkhand/Odisha (Singhbhum, Barbil-Barajamda), Karnataka (Kudremukh, Bellary), Goa, Chhattisgarh. Sketch India map outline + label states.
must
Lead-zinc deposits in India (Dec 2025 pattern)
Principal ore minerals: Galena (PbS), Sphalerite/Blende (ZnS), secondary — cerussite, smithsonite, anglesite. Major deposits: Zawar mine, Rajasthan — largest in India (Rajpura-Dariba, Mochia Magra), Rampura-Agucha (Rajasthan) — one of world's largest zinc mines, Sargipalli (Odisha). Formation: hydrothermal veins, SEDEX (sedimentary exhalative).
high
Contact metamorphism + Contact metasomatism (Or option)
Contact metamorphism: thermal effect of igneous intrusion on surrounding country rock → forms an aureole. Contact metasomatism (skarnification): chemical exchange between hot fluids from intrusion and carbonate host rock → skarn minerals (garnet, wollastonite, diopside, magnetite, epidote). Example zones: inner endoskarn, outer exoskarn.
good
💡 For economic geology: Indian state names + ore mineral chemical formulas + a rough India map = very strong answer. Examiners reward specific Indian location examples — drop at least 3 per answer.
