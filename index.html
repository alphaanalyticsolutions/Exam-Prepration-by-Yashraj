🧩 Q1 Cheat Sheet — Fill in the Blanks (All 6 Papers)
Superoxide dismutase is _______ enzyme.
✅ Metalloenzyme (antioxidant enzyme)
Synthesis of glucose from non-carbohydrate sources is _______.
✅ Gluconeogenesis
Increased levels of ketone bodies in blood = _______.
✅ Ketonemia / Ketosis
_______ salivary glands produce salivary amylase enzyme.
✅ Parotid (glands)
Fluid that separates after blood clots = _______.
✅ Serum
Functional unit of muscle fibre = _______.
✅ Sarcomere
_______ neurotransmitter deficiency causes Parkinson's disease.
✅ Dopamine
_______ hydrolyze ester bonds in fats.
✅ Lipases
Tiny uninsulated gaps in myelinated axon sheath are called _______.
✅ Nodes of Ranvier
Distance between two consecutive Z discs = _______.
✅ Sarcomere
Fructose-6-phosphate → Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate by _______.
✅ Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1)
Animals that excrete mostly uric acid = _______.
✅ Uricotelic animals (birds, reptiles)
_______ is a leucocyte with multi-lobed nucleus and phagocytic function.
✅ Neutrophil
Angiotensin-I enzyme (renin) is secreted by _______.
✅ Kidney — Juxtaglomerular cells
ADH is also known as _______.
✅ Vasopressin (antidiuretic hormone)
Acetoacetate is a _______.
✅ Ketone body
In relaxed muscle fiber, _______ blocks myosin binding site on actin.
✅ Tropomyosin
Stroke volume × Heart rate = _______.
✅ Cardiac Output
Glucose is phosphorylated to Glucose-6-phosphate by _______.
✅ Hexokinase (in muscle); Glucokinase (in liver)
A synapse close to a muscle cell is called _______.
✅ Neuromuscular Junction (NMJ)
Catecholamines are derived from _______ amino acid.
✅ Tyrosine
Calcitonin is secreted by _______ and reduces blood calcium.
✅ Thyroid gland (parafollicular / C-cells)
Cilia and flagella microtubules arranged in _______ configuration.
✅ 9+2 (nine peripheral + 2 central doublets)
Action potentials are conducted without _______.
✅ Decrement (non-decremental conduction)
_______ is formed in mitochondria from pyruvate oxidation.
✅ Acetyl-CoA
_______ are known as body building foods.
✅ Proteins
_______ is the non-protein organic molecule that binds non-covalently with apoenzyme.
✅ Coenzyme
Marine environment is _______ to invertebrates like sponges.
✅ Isotonic

🔥 Top 8 Topics — Study These Tonight

🧬 Enzymes

  • Isoenzymes — definition + LDH example
  • Allosteric enzyme — mechanism, effectors
  • Competitive vs Non-competitive inhibition
  • Enzyme regulation — feedback, covalent mod
  • SOD — metalloenzyme, reaction equation
  • GPx — selenium enzyme, GSH reaction
  • Km and Vmax significance

🍬 Carbohydrate Metabolism

  • Glycolysis — 10 steps, 2 ATP net yield
  • Glycogenesis vs Glycogenolysis (table)
  • Gluconeogenesis — substrates, site (liver)
  • TCA cycle — inputs/outputs per turn
  • Fate of pyruvate — aerobic vs anaerobic
  • HMP Shunt — significance

🥑 β-Oxidation of Fatty Acids

  • 4 steps per cycle: oxidation→hydration→oxidation→thiolysis
  • Products: FADH₂, NADH, Acetyl-CoA
  • Palmitic acid (C16): 7 cycles → 106 ATP
  • Carnitine shuttle for entry to mitochondria
  • Ketone bodies — types and conditions

💊 Vitamins

  • Fat-soluble: A, D, E, K — ADEK mnemonic
  • Vit A → Night blindness; D → Rickets
  • Vit E → Antioxidant; K → Blood clotting
  • B1(Beriberi), B2(Ariboflavinosis)
  • B3(Pellagra), B12(Pernicious anemia)
  • Vit C → Scurvy, collagen synthesis
  • Folic acid → Neural tube defects

💪 Muscle Physiology

  • Sarcomere: A-band, I-band, H-zone, Z-disc, M-line
  • Actin (thin) + Myosin (thick) filaments
  • Troponin-Tropomyosin system, Ca²⁺ role
  • Sliding filament theory — 8 steps
  • Cardiac vs Skeletal muscle — 6+ differences
  • Neuromuscular junction

🧠 Nervous System

  • Neuron parts: soma, dendrites, axon, terminals
  • Myelin sheath, Nodes of Ranvier, Schwann cells
  • Synaptic transmission — 7 steps
  • Resting potential (-70mV) vs Action potential
  • Membrane vs Action potential (table)
  • Key NTs: ACh, Dopamine, GABA, Serotonin

🩸 Blood & Heart

  • Haemoglobin: 2α+2β, heme+globin, Fe²⁺
  • RBC vs WBC comparison table
  • Plasma vs Serum difference
  • Heart: 4 chambers, valves, SA node
  • Cardiac vs Skeletal muscle table
  • O₂ dissociation curve factors

🔄 Amino Acid Metabolism

  • Transamination — ALT, AST enzymes, PLP coenzyme
  • Deamination — oxidative (GDH)
  • Urea cycle — 5 steps, 4 ATP cost
  • Peptides vs Proteins (table)
  • Excretory types: ammonotelic, ureotelic, uricotelic

📋 Exam Structure & Time Plan

Q1 — Compulsory (10 marks)

5 Fill in blanks (1 mark each) + 5 Match the following (1 mark each). Study the cheat sheet above — these are pure memory questions. Aim for 10/10 here.

Q2–Q7 — Choose ANY 4 (40 marks)

Each question = 10 marks. Usually 2 sub-parts: 5+5 or 3+7. Attempt the 4 you know best. Skip your weakest one.

⏱️ Time Plan (2 hours)

Q1: 15–20 min | 4 questions × 20 min = 80 min | Review: 20 min. Don't spend more than 22 min on any one question.

🎯 Score Target

Q1: 9-10/10 | Q2-Q7 (4 best): 8-9/10 each = 32-36/40. Total target: 41-46/50 for excellent marks.

🎯 5 Rules for Maximum Marks
1

Perfect Q1 = Free 10 marks: The fill-in-blanks and match questions are pure factual recall. Memorize the cheat sheet above. Even 8-9/10 here puts you in a great position. Spend 20 min max on Q1.

2

Prepare 6 topics, pick 4 best: From Q2–Q7, prepare: Enzymes, β-Oxidation, Vitamins, Muscle Physiology, Nervous System, Blood/Heart. That's 6 options to choose 4 from. Always skip your weakest.

3

Draw labelled diagrams everywhere: Questions on neuron, heart, sarcomere, β-oxidation get extra marks with a rough but labelled diagram. Even 2–3 minutes spent drawing is worth 2–3 marks.

4

Use comparison tables for "differentiate" questions: A 5-column table with 5 differences scores full marks faster than writing paragraphs. Pattern: Feature | Item A | Item B — repeat 5 rows.

5

Short notes formula (5 marks each): Definition (1 line) → Key features/parts (3–4 bullets) → Diagram or example (1) → Significance/function (1 line). That structure reliably scores full marks on short notes.
